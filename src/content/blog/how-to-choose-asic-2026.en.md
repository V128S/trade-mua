Choosing an ASIC miner comes down to a few key decisions: which coin to mine, what your electricity tariff is, and your budget. Let's go through each one so you don't overpay and buy hardware that actually pays back.

## 1. Pick the coin and algorithm

Every ASIC is built for a specific algorithm, and therefore for specific coins:

- **SHA-256** — Bitcoin. The largest and most stable market. This is the [Antminer S21/S23](/asic/sha256) line.
- **Scrypt** — Litecoin and Dogecoin at the same time (merged mining). See [Scrypt ASICs](/asic/scrypt).
- **KHeavyHash** — Kaspa, one of the fastest networks. That's the [Antminer KS7](/asic/kaspa).
- **Equihash** — Zcash. Dedicated [ASICs for ZEC](/asic/zcash).

Start with the coin you believe in for the long run — the rest of the configuration follows from it.

## 2. Assess your electricity tariff

The tariff is the main driver of profitability. A miner with great hashrate can run at a loss if power is expensive. Work out the cost of 1 kWh for your situation, and be honest about how many hours a day the hardware will run.

If your own tariff is too high, consider hosting in a [mining hotel](/services): an industrial tariff materially improves your margin.

## 3. Hashrate, power and energy efficiency

Don't chase hashrate alone. The key metric is **energy efficiency** (watts per unit of hashrate). Newer generations (S21, S23) deliver more hashrate per watt, so at the same tariff the profit is higher than with older models.

Compare models across all three together: hashrate, power and price.

## 4. Cooling: air, hydro or immersion

- **Air** — simpler to set up, suited to home and small farms.
- **Hydro** — higher hashrate and quieter operation, but needs cooling infrastructure.
- **Immersion** — for industrial sites and maximum overclocking.

Choose based on your hosting conditions, not the other way around.

## 5. Budget and payback

Always calculate payback before buying. Enter the hashrate, power, your tariff and the hardware price into the [profitability calculator](/calculator) — it shows daily profit and payback time at the live rate.
