Buying an ASIC is half the job. The second important question is where to host it. Let's compare two options — home and a mining hotel (colocation) — and when each makes more sense.

## Mining at home

**Pros:** full control, no hosting fees, hardware right next to you.

**Cons that are easy to forget:**

- **Noise.** An industrial ASIC is 70–80 dB, like a loud vacuum cleaner that never stops.
- **Heat.** A single miner puts out as much heat as a powerful heater — a problem in summer.
- **Power grid.** Household wiring isn't always rated for a sustained 3+ kW load.
- **Tariff.** A household tariff is usually higher than an industrial one, which directly cuts profit.

At home it's really only comfortable to run compact home models with low power draw.

## Mining hotel (colocation)

This means hosting your hardware at an industrial site. You remain the owner of the ASIC, while the operator handles the infrastructure.

**What you get:**

- **An industrial tariff** — lower than household, which directly improves your margin.
- **380V power** with a dedicated transformer and stable voltage.
- **Professional cooling** — no overheating in summer.
- **24/7 monitoring** and reboot on request.
- **Security:** 24/7 video surveillance and guards.
- **No noise or heat** in your home.
